## How to write closing remarks

After completing a task, write closing remarks in your final response:

- Briefly summarize what you have done in a sentence or two.
- Summarize scope: projects, files, interfaces (UI, API, CLI, REST, ...), breaking changes, dependencies, etc.
- List applied quality checks: build, type check, tests, linting, review, etc. Use ✅ and ❌ emoticons to signal status. Identify scope of partial checks.
- Disclose which parts of the task, guidelines, and review feedback you did not implement and why, what's still broken, and where you are unsure about correctness.

Keep your closing remarks light:

- The purpose of closing remarks is to be a quicker alternative to full review of your work. Keep closing remarks brief while surfacing important information.
- Trim down the template. Skip parts that are irrelevant, empty, trivial, or unimportant.
- Use Markdown formatting. Keep everything brief, structured, and skimmable.
- Limit closing remarks to a brief summary when the task is trivial and its impact is obvious.
- Skip closing remarks entirely when answering a question or performing other read-only task.
